<processing_pipeline>
You must perform these tasks in a single, combined pass:

1. TRANSCRIPT CLEANING & DEDUPLICATION: 
   Identify and remove all YouTube auto-transcription stutters, repeated words (e.g., "أيها الناس أيها الناس" -> "أيها الناس"), and filler speech patterns. Smooth out the stream-of-consciousness layout into logical sentences.

2. EXCLUSION OF IQAMAH & PRAYER:
   Stop processing immediately after the final prayers (Duas) of the second sermon (الخطبة الثانية). DO NOT translate, transliterate, or include the Iqamah (إقامة الصلاة), the prayer directions (استووا, تراصوا), the Quranic recitation in Salah, or the final Taslim (السلام عليكم ورحمة الله). The output must end exactly where the sermon ends.

3. CORRECTION OF TRANSCRIBED TEXTS:
   Correct distorted Quranic verses, Hadiths, names of Islamic scholars, Companions (Sahabah), and traditional terms that have been corrupted by automatic speech recognition (ASR) errors using standard Islamic references.

4. TRANSLATION & TONE:
   Translate the main Arabic discourse into clear, natural, and standard Malayalam prose. Ensure the tone is formal, respectful, and religiously appropriate for a Friday sermon (Khutbah).

5. ARABIC TRANSLITERATION & MEANING:
   - For all Arabic insert texts (Hadiths, Quranic verses, prayers, or Durood), TRANSLITERATE them phonetically into standard Malayalam Unicode script.
   - Immediately follow the transliterated Arabic text with its Malayalam translation/meaning, introducing it with the prefix: "അർത്ഥം: " (Meaning: ).

6. CALENDAR & NUMBER HANDLING:
   Convert all numbers, list indices, Hijri and Gregorian years, dates, and chapters/verses into full Malayalam words (e.g., "1448" -> "ആയിരത്തി നാനൂറ്റി നാൽപ്പത്തിയെട്ട്"). Never output digits (0-9, or Arabic/Urdu numerals).

7. EDITORIAL PARENTHETICALS:
   Incorporate any translator's notes or context smoothly into the Malayalam narrative flow.
</processing_pipeline>

<tts_formatting_rules>
Strictly apply these rules to ensure the output reads seamlessly for the Malayalam TTS engine:

Rule 1 - PURE MALAYALAM [HIGHEST PRIORITY]:
Every single character in the output must be in the Malayalam Unicode block, standard spaces, or basic punctuation (periods, commas, colons). Never output Arabic script, English letters, or foreign symbols. Transliterate all Arabic phrases phonetically.

Rule 2 - ZERO BRACKETS:
Never output brackets, parentheses, or braces: (), [], or {}. Integrate parenthetical notes directly into the sentence flow.

Rule 3 - ZERO NUMERALS:
Do not write any digit characters (0-9, or Arabic equivalents). Write every number, verse reference, and date fully in Malayalam words. 
- E.g., "(1)" -> "ഒന്നാമതായി," or "ഒന്ന്."
- E.g., "4" -> "നാല്"

Rule 4 - NO MARKDOWN OR SPECIAL CHARACTERS:
Remove all asterisks (*), hashtags (#), custom quotation marks (e.g. «, »), and bullet-point dashes. Use only standard Malayalam punctuation. Maintain double newlines between paragraphs to indicate natural speech pauses.

Rule 5 - EXPAND HONORIFICS:
Expand all Islamic honorifics into full phonetic Malayalam:
- 'ﷺ' / 'صلى الله عليه وسلم' -> 'സ്വല്ലല്ലാഹു അലൈഹിവസല്ലം'
- 'رضي الله عنه' -> 'റളിയല്ലാഹു അൻഹു'
- 'رضي الله عنها' -> 'റളിയല്ലാഹു അൻഹ'
- 'رضي الله عنهم' -> 'റളിയല്ലാഹു അൻഹും'
- 'رحمه الله' -> 'റഹ്മത്തുല്ലാഹി അലൈഹി'
- 'عز وجل' / 'سبحانه وتعالى' -> 'സുബ്ഹാനഹു വതആല'
</tts_formatting_rules>
